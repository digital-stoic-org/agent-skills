# BMAD v6 — Benchmark

**Date:** 2026-03-13 | **Source:** https://github.com/bmad-sim/BMAD-METHOD (36K⭐)

## What It Is

Full-ceremony, role-based agent methodology for software development. 7 named personas (Mary/Analyst, John/PM, Sally/UX, Winston/Architect, Bob/SM, Amelia/Dev, Quinn/QA) across 4 phases. Greenfield-optimized.

## Key Comparison

| Dimension | BMAD v6 | OpenSpec (Praxis) |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Ceremony | Full (4 phases, 7 roles) | Adaptive (skip what you don't need) |
| Greenfield projects | 🟢🟢🟢 (role handoffs shine) | 🟢🟢 |
| Brownfield/maintenance | 🟡 (over-ceremony) | 🟢🟢🟢 (garage mode) |
| Solo practitioner | 🟡 (roles feel artificial alone) | 🟢🟢🟢 (designed for solo) |
| Team contexts | 🟢🟢🟢 (natural role distribution) | 🟢🟢 (toothbrush = personal) |
| Token efficiency | 🟡 (role prompts are heavy) | 🟢🟢🟢 (SKILL.md + RTK) |
| Adaptive replanning | 🟡 | 🟢🟢🟢 (/openspec-replan) |
| Human gates | 🟢🟢 | 🟢🟢🟢 (gated delegation model) |

## Verdict

**Only framework that edges ahead — for greenfield team contexts.** BMAD's role-based phase handoffs work well when multiple people share the work. Praxis dominates solo practitioner and brownfield scenarios.

**Don't replace OpenSpec with BMAD.** Cherry-pick 3-4 BMAD innovations (role-based phases for greenfield, structured analysis templates).
